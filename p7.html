About Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh was born on 28th September in 1907. He was a revolutionary and freedom fighter who played a pivotal role in India's independence movement. Known for his unwavering commitment to the cause of freedom, he believed in armed struggle against British rule. His courage, idealism, and sacrifice made him an icon of the nationalist movement and continue to inspire generations of Indians.

Achievements

  • Founding member of Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA)
  • Participated in bombing of Central Legislative Assembly in 1929
  • Famous for protest writings and revolutionary ideas
  • Orchestrated the killing of John Saunders in retaliation for Lala Lajpat Rai's death
  • Advocated for a secular and socialist India
  • Executed by British on 23rd March 1931, becoming a martyred hero of independence
